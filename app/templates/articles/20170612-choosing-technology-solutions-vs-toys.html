Thoughts on You Are Not Google by Ozan Onay.

When choosing technologies, programmers need be wary that they focus on choosing the best tool to solve the problem, and not the "coolest" or the "newest" toy to play with.

Your goal should be to "solve" the problem mostly within the problem domain, not the solution domain.

Too often a technology is chosen because the bigger companies (e.g. Google) are using it.

... if you’re using a technology that originated at a large company, but your use case is very different, it’s unlikely that you arrived there deliberately; no, it’s more likely you got there through a ritualistic belief that imitating the giants would bring the same riches.

All aspects of a technology needs to be considered and not just the selling points. There are always trade-offs, but they are discussed far less often than the benefits.

Determine what was de-prioritized to achieve what was prioritized.

Even Google isn't Google ... What’s important is that you actually use the right tool for the job. Google knows this well: once they decided that MapReduce wasn’t the right tool for building the index, they stopped using it.

Ozan proposes the acronym UNPHAT as an aid for ensuring that a technology is being chosen for the right reasons:

  • Understand the problem before considering solutions.
  • eNumerate multiple candidate solutions for consideration, to avoid playing favorites.
  • Paper i.e. read each candidate's paper/documentation to understand what it was designed for.
  • Historical context in which the candidate was designed is important for understanding why it was created.
  • Advantages and disadvantages need to be weighed against each other.
  • Think (and really think!) about how well each candidate solution fits the problem at hand. Understand what would need to be different for you to change your mind.

Conclusion

Ensure that you're prioritizing solutions over toys when choosing a technology.

Use Ozan's UNPHAT acronym to double check yourself during the decision process.
